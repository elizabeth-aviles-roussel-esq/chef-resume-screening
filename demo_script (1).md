# Live Demo Script
## AI-Native Kitchen Hiring Workflow
### Version 2.1 | Duration: 60–90 seconds

---

## Before You Start

**What you need:**
- A laptop or tablet open to claude.ai (or the Resume Analyzer tool)
- The prompt template from `prompt_template.txt` copied and ready
- A real resume — ideally one the operator currently has on their desk

**The golden rule:**
Use their resume, not yours. If you walk in with a fake resume, it's
a presentation. If you run it on a resume sitting on their desk right
now, it's a revelation.

Ask before you sit down: *"Do you have a resume from someone you're
looking at right now? I want to show you this on a real one."*

---

## The Script

### Opening — 10 seconds

*While opening your laptop:*

> "Let me show you something that takes 30 seconds.
> I'm not going to tell you about it — I'm just going to do it."

Don't explain. Don't pitch. Just open Claude or the analyzer tool.

---

### Running the Analysis — 20 seconds

**If using the Resume Analyzer tool:**
- Drag their resume onto the drop zone — PDF, Word doc, or text file all work
- The tool extracts the text and sends it for analysis automatically
- Wait for the scored card to appear

**If pasting text:**
- Open the prompt template
- Paste the resume at the bottom
- Hit send

*While it runs:*

> "It's looking up every restaurant on this resume right now.
> The volume, the chef, the concept — all of it."

---

### The Reveal — 20 seconds

*When the output appears, don't narrate it. Let them read.*

Wait 5–10 seconds of silence. Then:

> "That took 30 seconds. It just researched every kitchen this person
> has worked in, scored their fit for your concept, and told you what
> one question to ask them if you bring them in."

Point to the score and the interview question specifically.
These are the two elements that land hardest.

---

### The Follow-Up — 20 seconds

> "You can run every resume in your stack through this. At the end,
> ask it to rank everyone — it'll tell you who to call first."

Then stop talking.

If they're engaged, they'll ask a question. Let them lead from here.

---

## Common Questions and Honest Answers

**"How does it know all these restaurants?"**
> "It's Claude — it's been trained on an enormous amount of text,
> including food media, reviews, chef interviews, all of it. It knows
> the difference between Le Bernardin and a hotel steakhouse."

**"What if the resume comes in as a PDF, Word doc, or just an email?"**
> "All three work. Drop the file into the analyzer tool — PDF, Word,
> plain text — and it extracts everything automatically. If it came
> in as an email, just copy and paste the text directly. Same result."

**"Does it work for non-NYC restaurants?"**
> "It knows restaurants nationally and internationally — though it's
> deepest on NYC. For another market, you'd adjust the benchmark
> restaurants in the prompt to match your local reference points."

**"Is this secure? Is my candidates' information safe?"**
> "The resume text goes to Anthropic's API — the same company behind
> Claude. The name and phone number fields in the tool never leave
> your screen — you type those in locally. I'd also recommend leaving
> out personal contact info from the resume text itself. The analysis
> only needs the work history anyway."

**"How much does it cost?"**
> "Claude.ai is $20/month for Pro. The analyzer tool is free to use
> once you have a Claude account. No per-analysis fee."

**"What if it gets something wrong about a restaurant?"**
> "It's not perfect — if it doesn't recognize a restaurant it'll say
> so, and you can ask it to search for more information. You're still
> the one making the call. This just saves you the Googling."

---

## The Close

Don't ask for anything in the demo. End with:

> "Want to run another one?"

If they hand you another resume, you've won the demo.
If they say "let me think about it," leave them the one-pager and
your contact. Don't push.

---

## New in Version 2.1 — What to Show If They're Ready to Go Deeper

If the chef or operator is clearly interested and has time, show them
the full workflow:

**The hiring inbox:**
> "There's a way to set this up so every resume that comes in — email,
> Craigslist reply, whatever — lands in one inbox. Then I can just ask
> Claude to read everything in the inbox and analyze all of it at once.
> No manual sorting."

**The file drop:**
> "For resumes that come in as attachments — PDF, Word doc, plain text
> — you just drop the file into the tool and it reads it and scores it
> automatically. No copying and pasting."

**Candidate tracking:**
> "Every resume you run gets added to a dashboard. Initials, date
> received, score, tier. You can add the name and phone number manually
> — those never go through the system, they stay on your screen only."

**The ranking:**
> "After you've reviewed ten or twenty resumes, you ask it to rank
> everyone. It gives you a top three, explains why, and tells you
> who to call first."

These four pieces — inbox routing, file reading, candidate tracking,
auto-ranking — are what turn a one-time demo into a repeatable
hiring workflow.

## Language Guide

**Say:**
- *"It looks up every restaurant"*
- *"It tells you what to ask them"*
- *"30 seconds"*
- *"Want to run another one?"*

**Don't say:**
- *"AI-powered"* — sounds like a pitch
- *"Machine learning"* — irrelevant to a chef
- *"Algorithm"* — creates distance
- *"Disrupting hiring"* — nobody asked
- *"Our platform"* — implies software they have to buy

---

## The One Thing to Remember

The demo works when the chef sees their own hiring problem solved
in real time.

It does not work when it feels like a product presentation.

Show, don't tell. Use real resumes. Let the output speak.
