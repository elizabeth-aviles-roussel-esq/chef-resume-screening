# Sample Analysis Output
## AI-Native Kitchen Hiring Workflow

This document shows example output from the hiring prompt applied to two
real candidate profiles. It is intended as a reference for what the
analysis looks and reads like in practice.

## Sample Candidate 1 — Score: 9/10 ✅ INTERVIEW

**Profile:** Lead Line Cook with French brasserie and upscale American background.
Currently at a high-volume Midtown operation.

---

**OVERALL FIT SCORE: 9/10**

**RELEVANT EXPERIENCE**
Current position is lead line at a French brasserie doing 200+ covers nightly in
Midtown — direct match to the target concept in both format and volume. Prior two
years at a high-volume upscale American institution in Midtown, with consistent
pace and strong covers. Six-month stint at a Daniel Boulud café adds Michelin-level
pedigree and technique exposure. The through-line is clear: this candidate has spent
the last five-plus years in precisely the kind of kitchen this role requires.

**RED FLAGS**
None. Timeline is clean, tenure at each role is substantial, and responsibility has
grown progressively. No gaps, no overlapping dates.

**CUISINE ALIGNMENT**
Excellent. French brasserie and upscale American cooking are the two pillars of the
target concept. This candidate has not just exposure but lead-line responsibility in
both formats. The Boulud café adds refinement without introducing a fine dining
tasting menu orientation.

**VOLUME EXPERIENCE**
Most direct volume match in any candidate pool reviewed. Both primary roles are
high-volume à la carte operations doing 200+ covers on busy nights. The candidate
has worked sustained pace, not just occasional busy services.

**LEADERSHIP LEVEL**
Ready for lead line immediately. Approaching sous chef territory based on tenure
depth and progressive responsibility. This candidate may require a growth path
conversation — they may be looking to move up, not laterally.

**KEY INTERVIEW QUESTION**
"At your current restaurant, what is your primary station during Saturday dinner
service, and how does the team manage communication and pace at 200+ covers?"

## Sample Candidate 2 — Score: 9/10 ✅ INTERVIEW

**Profile:** Sous Chef / Chef de Cuisine candidate with 14-year career spanning
Michelin-starred kitchens and Danny Meyer restaurants. Currently at a James Beard
Award–winning concept.

---

**OVERALL FIT SCORE: 9/10**

**RELEVANT EXPERIENCE**
Fourteen years across consistently serious kitchens. Garde Manger at a two
Michelin-starred MoMA restaurant. Four years at a high-volume Eataly restaurant
under a James Beard Award–winning chef. Two-plus years at a Danny Meyer Roman
trattoria at the Gramercy Park Hotel. Executive Sous Chef at a Greenwich Village
Italian restaurant with critical acclaim. Currently Lead Line at a James Beard
Award–winning Israeli concept. The career arc is one of the most coherent and
impressive in any candidate pool reviewed.

**RED FLAGS**
None. Exceptional consistency and trajectory throughout a 14-year career. No gaps,
no unexplained departures, clear progression at every stage.

**CUISINE ALIGNMENT**
Strong Italian and American foundations with high-level technique throughout.
The primary gap relative to this specific concept is French brasserie exposure —
this candidate has not cooked French specifically. However, the technique, volume
experience, and discipline from 14 years in serious kitchens transfers directly.
This is not a disqualifying gap; it is a training investment.

**VOLUME EXPERIENCE**
Eight of fourteen career years in genuinely high-volume kitchens. The Eataly
restaurant and Danny Meyer trattoria are both packed nightly operations with
serious cover counts. The current role is lower volume but high-pedigree.

**LEADERSHIP LEVEL**
Sous chef or chef de cuisine material based on career depth and breadth. The
Executive Sous Chef title at a respected independent concept is the strongest
leadership signal on the resume.

**KEY INTERVIEW QUESTION**
"You've spent most of your career in Italian-focused kitchens. How would you
approach transitioning into a French-American brasserie format at high volume?
What would your learning curve look like in the first 90 days?"

## Notes on Using These Samples

The above analyses were generated by Claude using the prompt in `prompt_template.txt`
applied to real candidate resume data. Restaurant names and specific pedigree details
were researched by Claude in real time — no manual Googling required.

Key things to notice:
- The analysis goes beyond job titles to assess the *significance* of each restaurant
- Volume is treated as a distinct dimension from pedigree or cuisine alignment
- Red flags (or their absence) are called out explicitly, not buried
- The interview question is specific to this candidate, not generic
- Leadership level is assessed honestly, including where a candidate may be overqualified

These samples represent strong candidates. For examples of lower-scoring analyses
(candidates with red flags, concept mismatches, or volume gaps), run the prompt
against any resume with overlapping dates, a heavy hotel restaurant background,
or exclusively fine dining tasting menu experience.
