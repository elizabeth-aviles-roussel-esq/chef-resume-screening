# Scoring Guide
## AI-Native Culinary Hiring Workflow | Version 3.0

This document explains how scores are assigned when analyzing resumes
for a Casual American restaurant in NYC with 80 seats.

---

## The 1–10 Scale

| Score | Label | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | **Hire** | Strong concept alignment, clean tenure, NYC pedigree at relevant restaurants. Call today. |
| 7–8 | **Strong Consider** | Solid background with minor gaps or partial alignment. Worth a phone screen. |
| 5–6 | **Consider** | Some relevant experience but meaningful gaps in cuisine, volume, or tenure. Proceed with caution. |
| 3–4 | **Pass (soft)** | Limited alignment. Would only revisit if the candidate pool is thin. |
| 1–2 | **Pass** | No meaningful fit. Do not pursue. |

---

## Scoring Dimensions

### 1. Cuisine Alignment (weighted most heavily)

**Strong signals for a Casual American concept:**
- Experience at Casual American, farm-to-table, or seasonal ingredient-driven restaurants
- Demonstrated familiarity with rotating or market-driven menus
- Scratch cooking — house-made stocks, charcuterie, butchery, pastry basics
- Farmer and purveyor relationships noted on resume or likely from concept type
- Experience at benchmark restaurants: Gramercy Tavern, Estela, Loring Place,
  ABC Kitchen, Olmsted, Vic's, The Dutch, Cafe Clover, Claudette, Lighthouse Brooklyn,
  Frenchette, Gage & Tollner, Maialino, Roman's, Marlow & Sons

**Neutral signals:**
- Italian-American, Mediterranean, or European bistro experience
  (transfers reasonably well; technique base is compatible)
- Fine dining tasting menu experience
  (technique is strong but pace and format may need adjustment)

**Weak signals:**
- Chain restaurant experience
- Hotel restaurant without a recognized chef-driven program
- Fast casual or corporate foodservice

---

### 2. NYC Kitchen Pedigree

The NYC independent restaurant market is specific. Candidates who have
cooked in recognized NYC kitchens understand the pace, the culture, and
the standards. Out-of-market candidates can be strong — but require
more due diligence on fit.

**Prioritize:** Named chef-driven NYC independents, recognized groups
(Major Food Group, Andrew Carmellini group, Danny Meyer group, etc.)

**De-prioritize:** Nameless NYC kitchens, national chains operating in NYC,
hotel F&B without a distinct culinary identity

---

### 3. Tenure Patterns

An 80-seat Casual American restaurant needs stability. Look for:

- **Green:** Tenures of 18 months or longer at relevant restaurants
- **Yellow:** 9–17 month tenures with a clear trajectory
- **Red:** Multiple tenures under 6 months without explanation;
  unexplained gaps of 6+ months; overlapping dates that need clarification

The restaurant industry has natural seasonality and project-based work —
context matters. Always read tenure in light of the role and the concept.

---

### 4. Leadership Trajectory

Match the candidate's level to the open role:

| Resume Evidence | Suggested Role |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Cook titles only, under 3 years experience | Line Cook |
| Cook titles, 3–6 years, lead or senior cook credits | Lead Line Cook |
| Lead cook or sous chef credits, 5+ years | Sous Chef |
| Sous chef or CDC credits, 8+ years, menu ownership | Chef de Cuisine |

Overqualified candidates (e.g., a former CDC applying for lead line)
should be scored with a note flagging the mismatch — they may be a
better fit elsewhere, or may be intentionally stepping back.

---

### 5. Volume & Format Fit

An 80-seat restaurant doing two seatings operates at roughly 100–160
covers per night. This is a focused, quality-over-quantity environment.

Candidates from very high-volume kitchens (300+ covers, hotel banquets,
stadium F&B) may find the pace of an 80-seat operation slow — or may
bring valuable efficiency and composure. Flag both possibilities.

Candidates from very low-volume kitchens (private chef, chef's table,
under 40 covers) may struggle with the consistency demands of 150 covers
nightly. Note the gap and probe in the interview.

---

## Automatic Disqualifiers

These do not require further analysis — score as Pass immediately:

- No restaurant kitchen experience whatsoever
- Resume shows only food retail, catering, or institutional food service
  without any restaurant line experience
- Candidate is currently under a non-compete from a direct competitor
  (rare but verify if senior role)

---

## Notes on AI Scoring

The AI score is a starting point, not a final decision. It is calibrated
to surface the most relevant experience quickly and flag risks clearly.
Human judgment is required before any candidate advances to an interview.

Factors the AI cannot assess:
- Demeanor, work ethic, and coachability
- References from prior employers
- How the candidate presents in person
- Whether their personality fits the team

Always conduct a phone screen before committing to an in-person interview.
