# Scoring Guide
## AI-Native Kitchen Hiring Workflow

---

## The 1–10 Scale

| Score | Tier | Action |
|-------|------|--------|
| 9–10 | Elite | Interview immediately — prioritize scheduling |
| 8 | Strong | Interview immediately |
| 6–7 | Consider | Worth a conversation — clarify gaps first |
| 4–5 | Limited fit | Line cook only, with reservations |
| 1–3 | Pass | Not a fit for this concept |

---

## What Moves a Score Up

**Volume match (+2 to +3)**
Direct experience at kitchens doing 150–300+ covers nightly in an à la carte format.
Restaurants like Le Rock, Monkey Bar, Oceana, Gallagher's, or any high-footfall NYC
brasserie or bistro. Evidence of sustained pace, not just busy weekends.

**Cuisine alignment (+1 to +2)**
French brasserie, upscale American bistro, or Italian trattoria backgrounds translate
directly. The cooking style at these concepts — composed proteins, seasonal vegetables,
house-made components — is directly applicable. Points awarded for depth and recency.

**Pedigree and progression (+1 to +2)**
Working under named chefs, Michelin-recognized kitchens, or James Beard Award–winning
teams is a signal of standards, discipline, and exposure to serious craft. Especially
valuable when combined with volume experience. Progression (growing responsibility
over time) is weighted more than title alone.

**Tenure and consistency (+1)**
Average tenure of 1–3 years per role suggests reliability and the ability to see a
season or concept through. Long tenures (4+ years at one restaurant) may indicate
deep roots in that concept.

**Leadership trajectory (+1)**
Clear movement from line cook → lead line → sous chef signals ambition and trust from
prior employers. Note: title inflation is common in small independent restaurants;
a "sous chef" at a 30-seat neighborhood spot is a different credential than a sous chef
at a 150-seat chef-driven restaurant.

---

## What Moves a Score Down

**Low-volume kitchens (−1 to −2)**
Hotel restaurants, private members clubs, tasting menu restaurants, and event catering
rarely expose cooks to sustained à la carte volume. The skills transfer in terms of
technique, but the pace does not. Deduct accordingly if volume is the primary need.

**Concept mismatch (−1 to −2)**
Heavy fast casual, bar food, or corporate catering backgrounds are less relevant.
Not disqualifying for line cook roles, but a significant gap for lead line and above.

**Short tenures without explanation (−1 to −2)**
Multiple stints under 6 months, especially without progression, raises reliability
concerns. One or two short tenures are normal (stages, seasonal gigs, a bad fit).
A pattern of them is a red flag.

**Date gaps (−1)**
Unexplained gaps of 3+ months warrant a direct question. Common causes are legitimate
(travel, family, injury) but should be verified in the interview.

**Overlapping dates (−1 to −2)**
Multiple jobs listed simultaneously without explanation (e.g., a full-time line cook
role and a full-time sous chef role at the same time) suggests resume inflation or
poor record-keeping. Flag for the interview.

**Generic formatting and copy-pasted bullets (−1)**
Resumes where every role has identical bullet points ("maintained cleanliness,"
"worked in team environment") suggest the resume was assembled hastily and may not
accurately reflect actual experience. Weight the restaurant names more than the
self-descriptions in these cases.

## Benchmark Restaurants by Tier

### Tier 1 — Direct Concept Match (High Volume + Upscale Casual)
Le Rock · Monkey Bar · Oceana · Gallagher's Steakhouse · Little Park
Happy Cooking group (Joseph Leonard, Perla, Bar Sardine, Fedora)
The Smile · Via Carota · Don Angie (brunch/dinner volume)
Quality Branded group (Quality Meats, Quality Italian)

### Tier 2 — Strong Pedigree (Fine Dining or Lower Volume)
Le Bernardin · Eleven Madison Park · The Modern · Gramercy Tavern
Per Se · Daniel · Jean-Georges · Aquavit
USHG group (Union Square Cafe, Manhatta, Maialino)

Upland · Loring Place · Charlie Bird · The Dutch

### Tier 4 — Limited Relevance
Hotel restaurants (without named chef) · Private clubs · Event catering
Bar food/gastropubs · Fast casual · Corporate dining

## Role-Specific Thresholds

| Role | Minimum Score | Volume Requirement | Leadership Signal |
|------|--------------|-------------------|------------------|
| Line Cook | 5+ | Some relevant exposure | None required |
| Lead Line Cook | 7+ | 2+ years in high-volume | Prior lead or senior line experience |
| Sous Chef | 8+ | 3+ years in high-volume | Prior sous chef or strong lead line |
| Chef de Cuisine | 9+ | Extensive high-volume | Proven leadership, menu ownership |

---

## Interview Question Bank by Red Flag

| Red Flag | Ask This |
|----------|----------|
| Fine dining only | "Have you worked a sustained 200+ cover à la carte service? How did that pace compare to what you've done?" |
| Short tenures | "Walk me through why you left [restaurant] after [X months]." |
| Date overlap | "I notice [Restaurant A] and [Restaurant B] overlap — were you doing both simultaneously?" |
| Low-volume kitchens | "What's the highest-volume service you've personally worked? What was your station?" |
| Title inflation | "At [restaurant], how many cooks were on the line during a typical dinner service, and what was your role relative to theirs?" |
| Cuisine gap | "You have a strong [Italian / Asian / etc.] background — how would you approach learning a French-American brasserie format?" |
| Career gap | "What were you doing between [date] and [date]?" |
